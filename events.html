A Day In The Life Of A Google Employee

On February 20, 2021, the IIC S.P.I.T in collaboration with Industry Relations Cell S.P.I.T conducted an online webinar - ‘A Day in the Life of a Google Engineer’, wherein the speaker, Mr.Prasad Pagade, a senior architect at Google gave us a peek into his thrilling life working at a top Silicon Valley company. Mr.Pagade graduated as a Bachelor of Engineering in Electronics from Mumbai University and completed his post-graduate studies in the field of Information Systems from Santa Clara University. Mr.Pagade has worked for corporations such as Infosys, Moody’s Corporation, Deloitte, Google and Looker. While having a large breadth of skills, Mr.Pagade’s chief interests lie in Data Analytics.

Mr.Pagade first gave us a deep look at one of the companies he’s working at, Looker - an online data platform where he is a senior consultant. Looker plays a vital role in real-time visualization of the data of a company, in order to spot trends and anomalies. A number of organizations have made use of Looker, including the Oklahoma State government, who utilized Looker’s services to use data intelligently in order to reduce the Coronavirus cases within the state.

Having given us a very deep insight into the Looker platform, Mr.Pagade shared with us a few thoughts in relation to how he built his career. “Always start small.”, he said, then adding - “Do not get bogged down by the incessant pressures of being a ‘topper’ or an ‘achiever’. Take your time with things but make sure you challenge yourself to see where your strengths lie.” He emphasized that making a note of your strengths and channeling your energy towards that is a key part of developing your career and personality as a professional. He then went on to iterate the significance of enjoying and cherishing our 4 years in engineering - from the great moments to the failures - all of it is pivotal in building fortitude of character. “ Sometimes, the little things we do may go a long way into developing the skills we need to be a great professional or entrepreneur. Playing cricket with my friends built my skills as a tactful negotiator, as funny as that sounds!”, said Mr.Pagade.



In response to some questions, Mr.Pagade described the impact of his postgraduate degree with reference to his career. He informed us of the different real-world projects and interactive classes he had the benefit of enjoying during his Master’s, including a capstone project he worked on in collaboration with a hospital, thereby seeing a real difference his work made. When asked about how he got employment at Google and what one needs to be a stand-out applicant, he said, “Be unique, innovative and true to yourself. Google looks for things beyond technical skills - they want to see leadership and creativity.”

Mr.Pagade then concluded the interaction by thanking the participants for their time and interest. The lively and inquisitive response of the 120 participants was just a testament to how insightful Mr.Pagade was and how enlightening the webinar was. “I’d always wondered what it took to be an engineer at Google. Now, thanks to this webinar, I have the facts and feel rather inspired to do what’s best for my career!”, said Pranav Kumar of SE-IT. The meeting ended with Maitri Vora, VP of Industry Relations Cell, SPIT, thanking the speaker Mr.Pagade for his time and brilliant insights.

Placement Bootcamp

On April 11, 2021, the IIC S.P.I.T in collaboration with Industry Relations Cell S.P.I.T conducted an online webinar - ‘Placement Bootcamp’. The speakers were Anukrit Jain, an investment banking analyst at JP Morgan and Azain Jaffer, an analyst at Deutsche Bank. Both the speakers are alumni of SPIT, each holding a B.Tech in Information Technology.

The first speaker was Azain Jaffer, who gave us a run-down of his experience with non-technical placements. Azain stressed the importance of good communication skills in building a competitive profile as it pertains to top placements. He spoke of the interviews, which are a great test of confidence and conviction. “Develop your skills and show confidence in yourself”, said Azain, as he detailed the key points interviewers look at - body language, confidence and articulate speech. “To be a standout candidate, you must engage in extra-curriculars, which are crucial in building your leadership and communication skills. Events held by clubs such as the debating society can help develop your public speaking skills.” Azain also noted that presenting research papers, participating in hackathons and cultural fests can also improve one’s public speaking. On being asked about how to acquire the relevant knowledge for a good financial role, Azain recommended the CFA level 1 certification syllabus and intangible knowledge gained from case study competitions. Azain also personally responded to a few questions directed at him.



The next speaker was Anukrit Jain, who was kind enough to break down the components of a great resume. He specifically emphasized the significance of “knowing your resume inside and out so as to answer questions based on it”. He shared his various experiences which he felt could be impactful on a resume and noted that not everything we do must be on the resume; rather it should be a summary of the most relevant experiences to the role or job in question. Anukrit also recommended the start-ups at SP-TBI as a great platform for gaining experience, be it technical or business-related. He had an important message about ‘broadening one’s horizons’ - “Open your mind to new avenues and take on new challenges outside of the college curriculum. This helps you build your profile and become an attractive candidate for organizations”. Anukrit also spent a few minutes while talking about the role of LinkedIn in today’s increasingly competitive and digital environment.

The audience was extremely invested in this talk as the speakers were very relatable and provided incredible insights. Over 100 students and faculty attended this webinar. “It has helped me understand more about non-technical placements and has encouraged me to engage in projects and events outside the classroom.”, said Arjun Pansare of SE-IT. The speakers were pleased with the engagement and active participation of the students, with Anukrit saying “Kudos to IR for organizing such a practically relevant event”. The meeting concluded with Chairperson of the IR-Cell Student Committee, Sayali Ayre thanking the speakers and attendees for their time.

Design Thinking

On December 18, the IIC S.P.I.T in collaboration with Industry Relations Cell S.P.I.T and E-Cell S.P.I.T, conducted its first event since the lockdown, a workshop on Design Thinking and Innovation Design. The speaker of the workshop was Mr.Sameer Chavan, a true industry expert with over 23 years of experience at leading product-based companies building a wide array of applications from desktop and mobile to hand-wearable.In addition to his Master’s degree from IIT Bombay,Mr.Chavan’s resume includes lead roles in ‘Design’ at Flipkart,Intel,LG and Siemens.

Design thinking is a popular and significant aspect of the process at any great product based company. Hence, Mr.Chavan stressed on the importance and true execution of design thinking. “Innovation and design goes hand in hand” - a rule to live by for any organization. Mr.Chavan alluded to the story of the Wright brothers to encourage the audience to understand that true innovation requires continuous effort, experimentation and the mentality to embrace failure as a learning opportunity. Mr.Chavan then went on to discuss an important question - ‘Why do some innovations fail while others are successful?’. This time he cited the example of Apple’s QuickTake, the world’s first consumer digital lens, which was way ahead of its time and did not have any impact on the average customer. “Story telling, sketching and prototyping are all examples of powerful visual thinking methodologies”, said Mr.Chavan. As companies are focusing more towards the aesthetics of their product, integrating visual and design thinking to an analytical, top down approach is the methodology of the future. Design thinking is a bottom-up, human centric process for innovation which encourages a ‘fail-fast’ method, keeping in mind the user experience. Mr.Chavan then went on to expertly break down the various steps an organization or individual must take to integrate a design thinking approach into the development process. ‘Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype and Evaluate’ are the pillars of design thinking. Essentially, the entire process revolves around human centric thinking, aesthetic simplicity,out-of-the-box problem solving and interaction between a corporation and its users. Mr.Chavan concluded his presentation and an interactive question-answer session was conducted, wherein he covered topics ranging from UX-design to project management with emphasis on design thinking ideology.



The workshop was attended by over 60 people and the feedback was overwhelmingly positive. “A very illuminating and unique workshop. I had never truly considered how crucial design-thinking is to the process of innovation!”, said Gaurav Parulekar of SE-IT. The meeting concluded with our principal, Dr. B.N Chaudhari and chairperson of the IIC, Dr.Kalbande offering Mr.Chavan their gratitude for sharing his expert insights and perspective with the students of SPIT.

Intellectual Property Rights

The Institute Innovation Council (IIC) S.P.I.T. organized a webinar on Introduction to Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) on 19th December 2020. The speaker invited was Prof. Kiran Talele who is an Associate Professor in the Electronics Engineering Department of Sardar Patel Institute of Technology. He is also the Chief Finance Officer & Head of Academic Relations, Sardar Patel Technology Business Incubator, Incharge of Innovation & Entrepreneurship Development Centre supported by Department of Science & Technology, Government of India.

The session began with him explaining the basic definition of property. He also explained in depth the different kinds of property and property rights. Then he delved into what exactly is intellectual property rights. He also spoke about different IPR types like patent, trademark, copyright, etc, and laid emphasis on the fact of how important these are to prevent someone from stealing one's brainchild. He took an example of dairy milk chocolate and explained the multiple IPRs which it holds. He then moved on to explain patents extensively.



He spoke about the salient features of patents and what kind of innovations can and cannot be patented. He spoke about the different acts which were passed by the government Of India over the years of patenting. Through an example, he explained hypothetically if we wanted to patent that particular product how do we go about it. He also threw light on the places where patenting happens and the charges of getting one's innovation patent. Similarly, he also shared what is copyright and the process and products associated with it. The viewers remained engaged from the start till the end and gained some valuable information through this webinar. They understood that in the future as entrepreneurs if they innovate something new what steps they need to follow to protect it through Intellectual Property Rights.
